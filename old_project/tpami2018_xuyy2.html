Multi-column CNN and its Applications for Crowd Counting and Face Alignment

Yanyu Xu    Shenghua Gao    Yingying Zhang    Yi Ma

ShanghaiTech University

Submitted to TPAMI (under review)

Abstract

This paper presents a simple but effective Multi-column Convolutional Neural Networks (MCNNs), which increases the width of the deep neural networks for feature extraction. We show that such MCNNs is closely related to some famous Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) architectures, including GoogLeNet, ResNet, DensNet, and ResNext. Then we apply our MCNNs to crowd counting and face alignment. We use CNNs with different sizes of kernels in different columns for feature extraction in these two tasks, which are psychology plausible because of the nature of these two tasks. Specifically, for crowd counting, we leverage MCNNs for density map regression, which helps the detection of heads with different sizes caused by perspective in an arbitrary image. For face alignment, we use face MCNNs to simulate the behavior of human in ground-truth annotation procedure where different facial key points prefer different sizes of local receptive fields, and even the same facial key point may prefer different sizes of local receptive fields in different images. Since exiting crowd counting datasets do not adequately cover all the challenging situations considered in our work, we have collected and labelled a large new dataset that includes 1198 images with about 330,000 heads annotated. Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness of MCNNs for both crowd counting and face alignment.
