Video Anomaly Detection with Sparse Coding Inspired Deep Neural Networks

Weixin Luo *     Wen Liu *     Shenghua Gao    Weixin Luo    Dongze Lian

ShanghaiTech University

Submitted to TPAMI 2018

Abstract

This paper presents a sparse coding inspired Deep Neural Networks (DNN) based anomaly detection method. Specifically, in light of the success of sparse coding based anomaly detection, we propose a Temporally-coherent Sparse Coding (TSC) where a temporally-coherent term is used to preserve the similarity between two similar frames. The optimization of sparse coefficients in TSC with Sequential Iterative Soft-Thresholding Algorithm (SIATA) is equivalent to a special stacked Recurrent Neural Networks (sRNN). Further, to reduce the computational cost in alternatively updating dictionary and sparse coefficients in TSC optimization and alleviate hyper-parameters selection in TSC, we stack one more layer on top of the TSC inspired sRNN to reconstruct the inputs, and arrive at an sRNN-AE. We further improve sRNN-AE in the following aspects: i) rather than using predefined similarity measurement between two frames, we propose to learn a data-dependent similarity measurement between neighboring frames in sRNN-AE to make it more suitable for anomaly detection; ii) to reduce computational cost in inference stage, we reduce the depth of sRNN in sRNN-AE and consequently our framework achieves real-time anomaly detection; iii) to improve the computational efficiency, we conduct temporal pooling over the appearance features of several consecutive frames for motion characterization, then we can feed appearance features and motion features into separate sRNN-AE for more robust anomaly detection. To facilitate the anomaly detection evaluation, we also build a large-scale anomaly detection dataset which is even larger than the summation of all existing dataset for anomaly detection in terms of both the volume of data and the diversity of scenes. Extensive experiments on both toy dataset under controlled settings and real datasets demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing methods, which validates the effectiveness of our sRNN-AE for anomaly detection.
