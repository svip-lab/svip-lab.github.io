DancingPose: A Simple Model for Generating Dancing Pose Sequence from Music

Yanyu Xu, Zhixin Piao, Peiyao Wang, Jiashi Feng, Shenghua Gao

Code Paper Supplemental Material

Abstract

In this work, we address an interesting problem, dancing pose sequence generation according to input music and initial dancing poses. The biggest challenges are how to generate high-quality dancing poses with dance style consistency and beat matching. To achieve this, we develop a simple model, named DancingPose, to exploit the correlations between the initial dancing poses and music audio, containing carefully designed Motion Encoder Module, Beat-guided Pacemaker Module and Audio-guided Attention Module. Specially, Motion Encoder Module uses the initial dancing pose sequences as references to keep dance style consistency. To impose the beat-matching property, Beat-guided Pacemaker Module only exploits the local correlations between foot speeds of the initial dancing and their corresponding auditory beats, while Audio-guided Attention Module considers the global correlations between the whole human parts and auditory features. To evaluate the prediction quality, we develop a new metric to quantitatively measure the beat matching degree of the predicted dance sequence. We have validated the effectiveness of our DancingPose model for predicting harmonic pose sequences with comprehensive experiments on an existing music \& dance.
